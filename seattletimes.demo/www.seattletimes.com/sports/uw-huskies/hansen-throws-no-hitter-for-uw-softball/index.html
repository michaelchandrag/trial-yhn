    Stevie Hansen throws no-hitter for Washington softball at Mary Nutter Collegiate Classic

    Feb. 20, 2025 at 10:38 pm
    By
    Seattle Times staff

    Stevie Hansen threw a no-hitter for the UW softball team against UC Riverside in a 10-0 win Thursday night in the nightcap at the Mary Nutter Collegiate Classic in Cathedral City, Calif.

    Hansen allowed no hits and no walks over six innings, with the only thing stopping a perfect game coming in the form of a defensive error. She struck out six Riverside batters.

    The Huskies (6-6) scored five runs in the first and won by 10 with just five hits. Riverside committed five errors in the game.

    Jadyn Glab went 2 for 2 with a home run and four RBI for UW. Alycia Flores also homered, going 1 for 3 with two RBI.

    In the first game of the day, the Huskies fell 3-1 to BYU.

    High school gymnastics

    • Ballard High School took home the 1A/2A/3A team state championship with an overall score of 173.750. Bainbridge placed second with 166.125 and Roosevelt came in third with 164.900.

    In the 1A/2A/3A all-around competition, Enumclaw’s Alysen Posey placed first with a score of 37.000. North Thurston’s Sadie McKune placed second, Shore­crest’s Berkley Gore came in third, Bainbridge’s Jade Bereman took fourth and Ballard’s Caitlyn Adams placed fifth.

    Men’s basketball

    • With a chance to get back to .500 in Big Sky play, the Eastern Washington men’s basketball team suffered its worst league loss of the season, 95-76, to Northern Colorado at Bank of Colorado Arena.

    The Bears (20-8, 12-3 Big Sky) shot a season-high 66.7% from the field and never trailed the Eagles (10-17, 6-8), who lost for the first time in February.

    “They are the best offensive team in the league, and today we were the worst defensive team, and that spells a tough day,” EWU head coach Dan Monson said in a postgame radio interview. “We just could never get a grip on them on the offensive end.”

    Senior Andrew Cook scored 21 points on 8-of-13 shooting to lead the Eagles, while sophomores Mason Williams and Sebastian Hartmann each added 13 points.

    • Seattle Pacific lost on the road 71-60 to Alaska Fairbanks. Owen Moriarty led the Falcons (14-13, 10-6 GNAC) with 20 points. Jaxon Nap had 15 points and a game-high 17 rebounds, and Jonas La Tour added 11 points.

    Advertising

    Women’s basketball

    • Northern Colorado (13-13, 6-9 Big Sky) ended the game on a 15-0 run as Eastern Washington (10-16, 6-8) went scoreless in the final 6 minutes, 29 seconds in a 50-46 loss at Reese Court in Cheney.

    The result moved the two teams into a tie in the Big Sky women’s basketball standings, each with a 6-8 league record. It also ended Eastern’s three-game winning streak.

    Peyton Howard, who scored a game-high 13 points on 5-of-19 shooting, missed a running layup in the final seconds that could have tied the game at 48. EWU freshman Kourtney Grossman, the Big Sky leader in rebounds, grabbed 16 of them against the Bears.

    • With 20-plus-point performances from Lolo Weatherspoon and Hunter Beirne, Seattle Pacific (8-20, 2-13 GNAC) won 73-67 on the road at Alaska Fairbanks.

    Beirne had a game-high six assists and scored 20 on 8-of-13 shooting from the field. Weatherspoon had four rebounds and two steals to go with her game-leading 21 points.

    Layne Kearns scored 17 and had five rebounds, and Madison Rubino had a double-double with 11 points and 13 rebounds.

    Baseball

    • After rallying with two runs in the top of the ninth inning to tie the game and another run in the 10th to take the lead, Seattle U surrendered a walkoff double to San Diego State’s Jake Jackson and fell 5-4 in extra innings. The Redhawks are 0-4 to start the season.

    Women’s swimming

    • Washington State’s Emily Lundgren placed first in the 200 individual medley with a time of 1:57.81, Darcy Revitt won the 50 freestyle with a 22.02 and WSU’s 400 medley relay team of Anna Rauchholz, Lundgren, Dori Hathazi and Emma Wright won with a time of 3:34.13 at the Mountain West swimming and diving championship in Houston.
